The Core Concept: The Vol Spring

Think of VIX term structure as a spring. Normally, VIX (30-day) trades below VIX3M (90-day) — this is called contango (healthy). When fear spikes, VIX rises above VIX3M — this is backwardation (stressed). The bigger the gap, the more compressed the spring.

The spring ALWAYS wants to return to normal. The question is when and how fast it snaps back. That snap-back IS the vol event rally.

NORMAL (contango): VIX 20 < VIX3M 22 overvix = -2.0 spring relaxed COMPRESSION: VIX 25 > VIX3M 24 overvix = +1.0 spring tightening VOL EVENT: VIX 32 > VIX3M 27 overvix = +5.0 spring fully compressed RELEASE: VIX 28 < VIX3M 27 overvix = +1.0↓ spring SNAPPING BACK → this is the rally

The Three Phases

Phase 1: Compression Building

What's happening: VIX is rising faster than VIX3M. The market is pricing in MORE short-term risk than long-term risk. Dealers are buying protection. Retail may be panic-buying puts.

Mechanically: As VIX rises, dealer hedging gets heavier. Negative gamma positions force dealers to sell into drops (amplifying the move). Vol buyers are winning — each point down creates more selling.

What you see on Volland:

  • SVB (Spot-Vol Beta) positive and rising — vol moving WITH price (abnormal)
  • DD flipping rapidly as spot crosses ATM strikes
  • Paradigm may be unstable (flickering between types)
  • Charm growing but being overwhelmed by selling pressure

Trading: Longs are dangerous. Our V12 filter correctly blocks non-SC longs (VIX > 22 gate). SC longs fire and mostly lose — this is the cost of being ready for Phase 3. Shorts work well.

Phase 2: Vol Event (Peak Compression)

What's happening: The spring is fully compressed. VIX is at extreme levels above VIX3M. This is where the experts start positioning for the reversal — NOT trading the reversal yet, but PREPARING.

Mechanically: At extreme overvixing, short-term options are massively overpriced vs longer-term. Market makers selling short-term vol (collecting premium) while buying long-term vol (hedging). The VIX futures curve inverts deeply. Any positive catalyst (headline, DD flip, vol seller pressure) can trigger a rapid unwind.

What you see:

  • VIX/VIX3M ratio > 1.10 (BigBill: "1.27 = extreme, SPX higher 7/8 times in 4 days")
  • Overvix > +2.5 (Wizard: "we need a SUPER overvixing")
  • Wizard's vol signal may fire (proprietary threshold, ~28/30 track record)
  • Community enters butterflies, calendars, VIX put spreads

Trading: This is NOT yet time to go long aggressively. The spring can stay compressed for hours or days. Expert positioning is via multi-day structures (butterflies, calendars) not 0DTE. SC still fires and may catch intraday bounces.

Phase 3: Vol Release (The Rally)

What's happening: The spring is releasing. Vol sellers take over. VIX starts DROPPING while SPX is flat or rising. The beach ball pops out of the water. Shorts get destroyed. Longs are incredible.

Mechanically: Vol sellers (institutional) step in to sell overpriced short-term vol. This pushes VIX down. As VIX drops, dealer hedges unwind — they STOP selling and start buying back, creating a positive feedback loop. Negative gamma flips to supportive flow. The drop in realized vol becomes self-reinforcing.

What you see on Volland:

  • SVB flips from positive → negative — THE key signal. Vol dropping while price rising = normal regime restored.
  • VIX "goes from green to red" (Phoenix's bottom signal)
  • DD may flip strongly positive (but this is consequence, not cause)
  • Shorts start losing — buyers absorbing all selling pressure

Trading: Go long aggressively. SC fires and wins at 90%+. All longs should be enabled. Wizard: "Undervixed rallies tend to run like a cartoon. It runs in place for a while and then takes off."

Why These Specific Thresholds?

Compression: Overvix > +1.0

+1.0

In normal markets, overvix oscillates between -2.0 and +0.5. When it crosses +1.0, VIX is meaningfully exceeding VIX3M — short-term fear is elevated beyond what the 90-day outlook suggests.

From our data (Mar 17-27): Average daily overvix ranged from -2.01 to +1.37. The +1.0 threshold was only crossed on Mar 27 (avg 1.37, peak 2.08) — confirming it catches genuine compression, not noise.

Why not lower? Overvix +0.5 happens on normal volatile days without compression mechanics. +1.0 filters out the noise.

Vol Event: Overvix > +2.5 OR VIX/VIX3M > 1.10

+2.5 / 1.10

This is based on Discord expert calibration:

  • Mar 27: Overvix peaked at 2.08, ratio 1.048. Both Wizard and Zack said "NOT a vol event." So 2.08 is below threshold.
  • Mar 6: Confirmed vol event. VIX 26.8-29.5. If VIX3M ~24 (estimated), ratio ~1.12-1.23. Overvix ~3-5.
  • BigBill: Ratio 1.27 = "extreme backwardation, SPX higher 7/8 times." We use 1.10 as "approaching" (lower than BigBill's extreme).
  • Wizard: "MASSIVE, not squeaker" — at ~2.0 overvix it's a squeaker. Needs +2.5 minimum for real vol event.

We use OR logic: Either overvix > 2.5 (spread-based) OR ratio > 1.10 with overvix > 1.5 (ratio-based). Different VIX levels create different overvix for same ratio, so both checks are needed.

Caveat: We only have 1 confirmed vol event in our data (Mar 6, no overvix tracking). These thresholds will be refined as we collect more data.

Release: SVB Flips from Peak > +0.5 to Below Zero

SVB flip

SVB (Spot-Vol Beta) measures the correlation between spot price and volatility. Normally it's negative (price down → vol up). During compression, it's positive (both moving together = stressed).

The flip from positive → negative is the moment vol sellers take over. They start pushing VIX down, breaking the correlation. This is the mechanical trigger for the rally.

Mar 9 proof: SVB went from +0.75 (09:45) to 0.03 (10:08) to -1.47 (10:57). The flip at ~10:30 marked the exact start of the rally. Longs before the flip: 3W/1L. Longs after: 8W/0L.

Mar 27 counter-proof: SVB went from +1.57 to +2.18. Never flipped negative. Vol sellers never won. No rally.

Requirements: SVB must have peaked > +0.5 first (confirming compression happened) AND dropped by > 0.5 AND gone below zero. This triple condition prevents false signals on normal negative-SVB days.

Key Principles

Principle 1: Overvix level is the SETUP, not the TRIGGER. It tells you the spring is compressed. It does NOT tell you when it releases.
Principle 2: The SVB flip from positive → negative IS the trigger. It means vol sellers took over. This is measurable and detectable in real-time.
Principle 3: The compression can last hours or days. Don't try to time the exact bottom during P1/P2. Instead: accept SC losses during compression (cost) and ride the release (payoff). Cost/reward: 1:4.5.
Principle 4: A "squeaker" (overvix +2) is NOT a vol event. You need "MASSIVE" (+3-5) for high conviction. But even squeakers can resolve with small bounces — SC catches these.
Principle 5: After confirmed vol events, the NEXT DAY is the best trading day of the month. Position overnight (via multi-day structures) for maximum capture.
