The single most important level this week is 6800 SPX (6837 ES). Here's why.
What 6800 SPX Positive Gamma Means for You
At 6800 SPX, dealers (the banks and market makers on the other side of your options trades) hold a massive concentration of positive gamma — between $81 million and $122 million worth across every expiration window we track. In practical terms, this means:
When price dips toward 6800: dealers are mechanically forced to BUY — they must hedge their exposure by purchasing futures. This creates an invisible cushion under the market. When price rallies away from 6800: dealers are mechanically forced to SELL — locking in their hedge gains. This creates an invisible cap above. The combined effect: 6800 SPX acts like a magnet and a shield simultaneously. Price wants to stay near it. Violent moves away from it tend to get pulled back.
This is likely what contained Friday's selloff. After the CPI spike to 6888, price faded but only reached 6846 ES (6809 SPX) — it never actually tested the 6800 SPX gamma wall directly. The gamma cushion may have acted as a gravitational floor that prevented the selloff from extending deeper, even without a direct tag.
The only thing that breaks this cushion is a VIX spike large enough to overwhelm it — and that's the Iran headline risk scenario. If VIX suddenly jumps above 23 on a major escalation, the gamma cushion can be overwhelmed, and 6800 breaks.
