**ALWAYS assess DD alongside PnL — both are equally important. Never present one without the other.**

**Why:** In real money, it means nothing if you make 1M points if the first DD is -1000 points — the account gets liquidated before you can claim the profit. Risk management is ALWAYS the priority. Real accounts have limited capital ($2K for 1 MES). A -45pt red streak = 22.5% drawdown = margin call / liquidation risk.

**How to apply:**
- ALWAYS show MaxDD alongside PnL in EVERY filter comparison, backtest, and study
- ALWAYS illustrate risk: worst day, max consecutive losses, DD duration, recovery time
- Use PnL/MaxDD ratio as primary comparison metric (higher = better risk-adjusted)
- A filter with less PnL but lower MaxDD is BETTER for real money
- When a filter blocks some winners but eliminates red streaks: that's a GOOD trade-off
- In tables: put MaxDD column right next to PnL, not hidden at the end
- In recommendations: lead with risk profile, then show return
- Never recommend a filter purely on PnL without showing what the worst case looks like
- Example: V10 was -21 pts behind V9-SC but had all-green trades on a day V9-SC had -45pt DD before recovering. V10 is the better filter for real money.
