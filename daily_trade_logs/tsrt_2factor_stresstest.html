🧪 2-Factor Sizing — Honest Stress Test

Deep validation per request: defensive-only half, all-months concentration, Jun 3 vs Jun 4 with bar-by-bar gamma, and a cap-aware sim. Verdict: the edge is FRAGILE — concentration-dependent, regime-specific, and weaker than the headline numbers.

2 · The full edge is concentration-dependent

Full 2-factor, all months, no look-ahead: uplift +$3,523 over 71 days — but:

  • Top single day = 56% of the entire uplift. Remove the top 3 days → only +$381 left.
  • 35 negative days vs 33 positive — it loses on the majority of days, wins big on a few (positive-skew lottery).
  • Real TSRT post-V16: Jun 4 alone = 59% of the uplift; minus top 3 days = −$235 (negative).

3 · Why Jun 4 won and Jun 3 lost — it was the TAPE, and gamma is unreliable

Both days had similar (flat-to-green) semis, so the sizing 2×'d the longs both days. The difference was the price action — and bar-by-bar gamma exposes a flaw:

Key flaw exposed: my gamma rule boosts longs 1.25× when gamma is negative ("squeeze fuel"). But Jun 3 was negative-gamma all day and longs lost — negative gamma amplified the DOWN move, not up. Negative gamma isn't bullish — it amplifies whichever way the tape is already going (which we can't know in advance). So the gamma factor is unreliable bar-by-bar; the daily "neg-gamma → longs win" was an aggregate artifact.

Verdict

After full stress-testing: the 2-factor sizing is NOT a robust ~1.5× edge.
  • Defensive half alone = net loser (June-only).
  • Full edge = concentrated in a handful of days (top day 49–59%), more losing days than winning.
  • Gamma factor = unreliable bar-by-bar (negative gamma amplifies both directions).
  • The $300 cap doesn't rescue it (slightly hurts).
The most defensible read: it's a positive-skew bet that pays on rare big trend-green days (Jun 4) and bleeds the rest of the time — net-positive in these samples only because of a few outliers. Not safe to scale real money on; the semi signal's real (small) value is regime-dependent, concentrated, and needs long forward-validation.
