Mechanism — refined

GEX-TARGET = price is AT a +GEX magnet strike. Per Volland framework, this means price has reached its destination.

AM phase: open near +GEX magnet → brief gamma-squeeze pop. Price gets pulled up to the target. Long entries ride the magnetic pull. → +6.88pt mean, 73% WR.

PM phase: price has been at/near magnet for hours → destination exhausted, no more upward pull. Long entries are chasing a destination that's already been reached. Mean reversion / stabilization kicks in. → −8.33pt mean, 0% WR over 6 trades.

Hypotheses tested and rejected

1. Blanket GEX-TARGET block. Would lose +75.7pt AM winners to save +50pt PM losers. Net +$28 only across 12 trades — not worth the complexity.
2. Paradigm-shift trigger (block N min after intraday paradigm change). REFUTED. Fresh shifts (0-15min) = +2.17pt mean, normal. Not a danger signal at all.
3. General "PM longs bad" pattern. REFUTED. BOFA-PURE keeps +3.27pt PM, AG-TARGET is +9.93pt PM. Other paradigms also fine. GEX-TARGET is the standout.
4. DD-Exhaustion-only GEX-TARGET block. Saves only +$180. DD's losses are already in the PM cluster — the PM rule captures them plus the SC/ES Abs PM losses too.
