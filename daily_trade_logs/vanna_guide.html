⚡ Vanna — How It Works & How To Trade It

Synthesized from the Volland white paper + Discord community (Wizard of Ops, Apollo, Dark Matter, official 0DTE alerts). The one rule to remember: vanna is a magnet — but a VOL-CONDITIONAL one.

1 · What vanna is

Vanna = how much option deltas change when implied volatility (IV) changes. Equivalently, how much vega changes when spot moves. Volland measures dealer vanna exposure (in $ notional) at every strike.

"From a delta lens, vanna only has an effect when IV changes." — Johannes (Volland)

That last point is the whole game: vanna is a VOLATILITY greek, not a spot greek. On a quiet day where IV barely moves, vanna does little. It "wakes up" when vol moves — or, more subtly, as IV slowly bleeds toward zero into expiration (the "vanna drift").

2 · The core rule (and why it flips)

The naive starting rule everyone learns:

Positive (green) vanna = MAGNET / support  ·  Negative (red) vanna = REPELLENT / resistance

This is true only in the normal / low-vol regime (VIX flat or falling — which is most of the time, because VIX is usually in contango and near-term IV is bleeding out). The instant VIX starts rising (stress), the polarity inverts. This is the single biggest mistake new traders make.

The table the community actually uses

Normal vol (VIX flat/falling — most days)Rising / high vol (VIX up, stress)

"VIX down → target positive vanna ABOVE spot. VIX up → target positive vanna BELOW spot." — the daytrading-central one-liner

3 · Why it's vol-conditional (the gotcha)

Wizard of Ops confirmed the definitive version directly in Discord:

During low volatility (VIX flat/falling — the majority of the time), positive vanna — whether below OR above spot — is BULLISH. During increased volatility (VIX rising), positive vanna below spot is actually BEARISH — it contributes to a faster downside move. — confirmed by Wizard of Ops
  • Vanna drift: because IV → 0 into expiration, a positive-vanna book has a built-in upward drift just from time passing on quiet days. That's the structural bullish bias of normal days.
  • Apollo's confirmation tell: a vanna support "really plays out" only when the strike's IV actually falls as price approaches. Watch the key strike's vol — rising = support fails, falling = support holds.
  • Polarity inversion (Dark Matter, VIX>~20): "positive vanna above spot becomes resistance rather than magnet; negative vanna below spot becomes structural support."

4 · The level concepts

  • Vanna magnet / attractor — a strong +vanna concentration that pulls price toward it in normal vol. (See today's live example below.)
  • Vanna support / resistance — the workhorse. Official alerts quote a specific weekly-vanna strike as the next S/R band beyond the 0DTE edges every morning (e.g. "7320 is our vanna support for the week").
  • Vanna wall — a large cluster, often multi-expiry confluence, that caps/floors price (e.g. a triple-negative gamma+vanna+delta wall dealers "could not clear").
  • Vanna flip / zero-cross — the strike where per-strike vanna changes sign; treated as a fade pivot. "When all the negative vanna flips positive" → breakout fuel above.
  • Cascade — breaching a −vanna-below zone is self-reinforcing: "these reds start flipping and cascading the sell" → fast slide once support breaks.

5 · Which expiry to use

Unlike charm (always 0DTE), vanna is read multi-expiry. Match the tenor to your holding period:

  • Intraday S/R → weekly vanna ("vanna support for the week").
  • Swing / multi-day targets → monthly / all-expiry vanna walls (the persistent magnets).
  • Short-term vanna is more influential for near-term price; persistent walls live in monthly. Different tenors can conflict (a longer-term resistance can sit below a shorter one) — don't mix them.

6 · Vanna vs gamma (mental model)

Gamma = structure (pin/accelerant from spot moves) · Vanna = volatility bias (where vol takes price if vol moves) · Charm = time flow · Delta = directional conviction

Gamma walls hold when VIX is stable (intraday pins). Vanna governs the vol-driven excursions: "couple vanna with vol — IF we get a vol spike, what area does vol take us? I use the vanna levels."

7 · How to trade it — practical rules

  1. Default (normal vol): treat the nearest strong +vanna as a magnet/target and the nearest −vanna as resistance. Bias long toward +vanna above; expect drift up into expiry.
  2. Check vol direction FIRST. If VIX is rising, INVERT everything — fade rallies into +vanna above (now resistance), expect +vanna below to accelerate downside.
  3. Confirm with the strike's IV: as price nears a vanna level, is that strike's vol falling (support holds) or rising (it fails)?
  4. Fade the vanna flip on normal days; play the cascade (continuation) when a −vanna-below zone breaks cleanly.
  5. Confluence = conviction: when 0DTE + weekly + monthly vanna stack same-sign within a few points, that wall is breached far less often — high-confidence S/R or target.
  6. Line it up: for a directional thesis, make sure charm, vanna (and sometimes gamma) all agree before sizing up.

8 · Live example — Jun 11, 2026 (the 7400 magnet)

SPX opened 7289, ran to a high of 7411, and closed 7396 — +107 points, drawn straight up to the 7400 +vanna magnet (+215M cluster) and pinned there.

VIX was ~21.5 but falling (post-war-headline relief) → normal-vol magnet behavior held: positive vanna above spot acted as the upside magnet, and IV bleeding out provided the drift. Textbook "vanna drift → magnet → pin." Had VIX been rising instead, that same 7400 +vanna would have acted as resistance and price would have been sold into it.

Summary

Vanna tells you where vol will take price. In the normal regime (VIX flat/falling, most days) +vanna is a magnet and price drifts into it as IV bleeds out. When VIX rises, the polarity inverts and the same +vanna becomes resistance/accelerant. Read it multi-expiry (weekly for intraday, monthly for swing), confirm with the strike's IV, and always check vol direction before trusting the sign.
